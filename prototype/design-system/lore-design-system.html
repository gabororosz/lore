Foundations · brand mark

The L is a merge

An L is two lines meeting at a corner — exactly what Git does when it merges two branches into one. The mark leans in: a branch (docs-sync) curves into the main stroke and settles at the bright source node, where the single source of truth lives. Color follows the storage system — link, derived, source.

Foundations · color

Color carries meaning

In Lore, a hue is not decoration — it names where a piece of knowledge lives and how much to trust it. The three storage neons come straight from the architecture: source, derived, transient. Two more carry connection and conflict.

Source#34F5B4

Published truth. Lives in Git — the single source of truth. Also the color of the Publish action.

Derived#35D6FF

Rebuildable cache — index, links, search, the /docs mirror. Safe to discard and rebuild.

Transient#FFC24B

Drafts in flight. On the server, on the way to Git. Short-lived by design.

Link#B98BFF

Wiki-links, AI, and brand. The connective tissue of the knowledge graph.

Conflict#FF5C7A

Merge conflicts and errors — the one moment where Git surfaces to the reader.

Foundations · voice & tone

Words are design material

Plain verbs, sentence case, no filler. Say what happens; keep the same name through the flow. Errors explain and point forward — they don't apologize.

Publish   Submit

Name the outcome, not the mechanism. The button that says Publish produces a toast that says Published.
